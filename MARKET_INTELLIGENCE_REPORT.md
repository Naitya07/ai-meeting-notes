## Part 3: Global Market & Regulatory Map

### Region-by-Region Analysis

#### North America (US + Canada)
- **Market:** Largest and most mature. $23.4B conversation intelligence TAM.
- **Recording Laws:**
  - US: 39 states = one-party consent. 11 states = ALL-party consent (CA, FL, IL, MD, MA, PA, WA, etc.)
  - Illinois BIPA: Voiceprints are protected biometric identifiers. $1K-5K per violation. MeetingMind's on-device processing avoids BIPA entirely since no biometric data is transmitted.
  - Canada: One-party consent federally (Criminal Code s.184). PIPEDA applies. Quebec Law 25 adds stricter requirements.
- **Entry Priority:** IMMEDIATE. Start here.

#### Europe (EU + UK)
- **Market:** 27.8% of global higher ed market. Strong enterprise SaaS adoption.
- **Recording Laws:**
  - GDPR: Voice data = personal data. Requires lawful basis (consent or legitimate interest). DPIAs mandatory for AI processing employee data.
  - EU AI Act: HR AI = high-risk (effective Feb 2025). Requires AI literacy training, human oversight, transparency.
  - Germany (BDSG): Strictest in EU. Employee monitoring heavily restricted. Works councils have veto power.
  - France (CNIL): Requires explicit employee consent for recording.
  - UK GDPR: Similar to EU but separate regulatory body (ICO).
- **MeetingMind advantage:** On-device processing = data minimization by design (GDPR Art. 5). No cross-border data transfer issues.
- **Entry Priority:** Year 1-2, after EU localization.
